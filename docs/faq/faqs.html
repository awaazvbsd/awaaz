‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

1 Why did you choose this problem?

"Because many students experience stress silently. We wanted to find a way to notice distress early‚Äîwithout forcing students to speak up or label themselves."

2 Aren't schools already addressing mental health?

"Yes, schools are doing their best through counselling and awareness. AWAAZ strengthens these efforts by adding an early, objective signal that can be easily missed."

3 What is the core scientific idea behind AWAAZ?

"Stress causes involuntary changes in the human voice‚Äîlike pitch, breathing, and muscle tension. These changes can be measured and used as a digital biomarker for early stress."

4 How do we know this is science and not just tech buzz?

"We analyse measurable acoustic features, not emotions or opinions. Voice-based stress research is well-established; AI is only used to process these signals efficiently."

5 Why voice instead of surveys or self-reporting?

"Surveys depend on honesty and comfort. Voice changes happen involuntarily, even when students are unaware or unwilling to share."

6 Why not wearables or biological tests?

"They are costly, invasive, and impractical for large school populations. Voice uses what students already do naturally‚Äîtalk."

7 How exactly does AWAAZ work (simple architecture)?

"Short voice samples ‚Üí acoustic feature extraction ‚Üí stress pattern analysis ‚Üí anonymised indicators ‚Üí counsellor alerts. No raw audio is stored."

8 Does AWAAZ diagnose mental illness?

"No. AWAAZ does not diagnose. It only provides early indicators to help adults decide if support is needed."

9 What if the system flags stress where there is none?

"AWAAZ never acts alone. Alerts are reviewed by teachers or counsellors, so human judgment always comes first."

10 Why include digital questionnaires if AWAAZ is voice-based?

"Voice is the primary signal. Questionnaires are optional and only add context when counsellors feel it's needed."

11 How do you protect student privacy?

"No raw voice recordings are stored. Data is anonymised, minimal, and accessible only to authorised wellness staff."

12 India has many languages. Will this work everywhere?

"Yes. We analyse how a student speaks, not what they say. Stress-related voice changes are language-independent."

13 How does AWAAZ fit into daily school routines?

"It works during normal classroom activities and uses existing devices‚Äîno extra periods or disruption."

14 How does this align with Government of India initiatives?

"AWAAZ supports NEP 2020, UMMEED, MANODARPAN, Tele-MANAS, and the Supreme Court's 2025 guidelines by enabling early, stigma-free detection."

15 Are you saying existing initiatives are insufficient?

"No. They provide the framework. AWAAZ amplifies them by offering a practical, scalable early-detection tool."

16 How do teachers benefit from this?

"Teachers receive simple indicators, not complex data‚Äîhelping them notice students who may need attention earlier."

17 How do you measure success beyond detection?

"Earlier interventions, faster support, reduced escalation, and healthier classroom trends over time."

18 Can this scale beyond one school?

"Yes. It's low-cost, device-light, and suitable for schools, colleges, and coaching institutes."

19 What stage is the project at now?

"We have a working proof of concept and are discussing a pilot with our own school, which helps us test responsibly."

20 What makes AWAAZ innovative?

"It transforms everyday speech into a non-invasive wellness signal‚Äîquietly, ethically, and at scale."
